THE TEXAS CONSTABLE:
Know Your Rights

"A summons is an invitation to court, not an order to leave."

In Texas, the Constable's primary role in an eviction is to serve legal notice. Many tenants feel forced to move the moment a badge appears at their door. This is a mistake.

You have the right to a hearing, the right to see the evidence against you, and the right to appeal. The Constable is an officer of the court, but they are not your judge.

Stay Dangerous. Stay Informed.

Texas Summer: Is a Broken AC an Emergency?

Texas Property Code § 92.056

In the Baytown heat, a broken AC isn't just an inconvenience—it can be a health hazard. While the law doesn't explicitly say "AC must be 72 degrees," it does say landlords must repair conditions that affect the **physical health or safety** of an ordinary tenant.

THE TRICK:

If you have a medical condition, or if the heat is reaching dangerous levels (90°+ inside), you have a stronger case. Always send your notice via **Certified Mail** to start the legal clock.

Mold: The Silent Evictor

Don't let them paint over the problem.

Landlords love to use "KILZ" paint to hide mold. Don't fall for it. Mold is usually a symptom of a leak. Under Texas law, if the mold is caused by a leak they failed to fix, they are responsible for the remediation.

ACTION STEP:

Take clear photos. Use a humidity meter (you can get them for $10). Document every cough or headache. Your health is not negotiable.

Can They Evict Me for Complaining?

Retaliation is Illegal (TPC § 92.331)

Many tenants are scared to use our Generator because they fear the landlord will kick them out. Texas law protects you from **Retaliation** for 6 months after you file a formal repair request.

THE SHIELD:

If they try to raise your rent or give you a notice to vacate right after you ask for repairs, they are likely breaking the law. Keep your "Stay Dangerous" notice as proof that you exercised your rights first.
